Photography Book Series


This series explores personal trauma through photography and text, transforming images into letterforms and numbers to emphasize the relationship between dreams, memory, and digital abstraction. Each book presents a conceptual narrative where images progressively shift in clarity as you flip through, reflecting themes of change, instability, and identity.

I DON’T WANT TO CHANGE…

This piece visualizes my fear of change, inspired by a recurring dream. It depicts a bathtub scene where warmth fades into an icy prison, symbolizing resistance to transformation until stagnation becomes unbearable.

PARADISE: LOST.

Exploring my fear of instability, this project is rooted in childhood memories of moving frequently. A recurring dream of returning to a former home, only to find it in ruins, reflects the emotional loss tied to displacement.

PLEASE, DON’T GO.

The final piece visualizes a dream of my father walking away with a suitcase, symbolizing my fear of abandonment. This dream surfaced when I confronted my sexuality, reflecting anxieties around rejection and identity.
