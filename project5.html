Glitch Poster Project

This project was supposed to be an "exploration of digital disruption," but honestly, it was my excuse to mess around with corrupted files and broken screens. The assignment was all about embracing imperfection—leaning into digital errors rather than fixing them.

The manifesto behind glitch art is kind of existential: Why are we obsessed with perfection? Why do we keep upgrading everything? My approach was less theoretical and more "let’s see what happens when I push this image to its breaking point." Turns out, some of the best moments come from total failure.

The final piece came from a combination of over-compressing images, messing with color channels, and literally opening a file in the wrong program until it broke. If that’s not an artistic process, I don’t know what is.
